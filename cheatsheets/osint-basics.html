OSINT Basics: Getting Started

ThreatNet Team July 23, 2025 OSINT, Basics, Reconnaissance

Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT) is the collection and analysis of information gathered from open sources (publicly available sources) to produce actionable intelligence. It's crucial for cybersecurity, investigations, and threat intelligence.

Key Principles of OSINT

  • Legality: Only collect data that is publicly available and not protected by privacy laws.
  • Attribution: Always note the source of your information.
  • Verification: Cross-verify information from multiple sources to ensure accuracy.
  • Operational Security (OPSEC): Be mindful of your digital footprint during investigations. Use tools like VPNs, Tor, and virtual machines.

Fundamental OSINT Categories

1. Web Search Engines (Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo)

Mastering advanced search operators (Google Dorks) is fundamental.

site:example.com intitle:"admin login"
filetype:pdf "confidential"
inurl:backup "database"

2. Social Media Intelligence (SOCMINT)

Platforms like Twitter, LinkedIn, Facebook, and Instagram can yield vast amounts of personal and organizational data. Tools like Maltego can visualize connections.

  • Profile analysis (usernames, bios, connections)
  • Post content (location data, sentiments, activities)
  • Image analysis (EXIF data, reverse image search)

3. Public Records & Databases

Government registries, business registration databases, academic papers, and court records are rich sources.

  • WHOIS lookups for domain ownership
  • Company registration details
  • Property records

4. Geolocation OSINT

Identifying physical locations from photos, videos, or public posts.

Tools:
    - Google Maps/Earth
    - Wikimapia
    - SunCalc

Recommended Basic Tools

  • Search Engines: Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo, Yandex
  • WHOIS Lookup: who.is, ICANN Lookup
  • Reverse Image Search: Google Images, TinEye, Yandex Images
  • Social Media Search: OSINT Framework, custom searches
  • Archiving: Wayback Machine

Always document your findings and methodology to ensure reproducibility and accountability.
